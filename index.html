Hello there,
Welcome to my corner of the internet.

I'm Rushali, an AI enthusiast currently navigating the realm between technology and humanity. I am an ardent advocate for human-centered machine learning, dedicated to fostering trust between individuals and AI systems.

I am a first-year Master’s student at Northeastern University, Boston. Under the guidance of Samuel Scarpino, I am engaged in predicting disease outbreaks and simulating epidemic strategies, using Large Language Models, at the Institute for Experiential AI. My current research interests revolve around eXplainable AI, machine learning interpretability, and the examination of biases within AI systems.

Previously, I was an R&D Engineer at ArtPark, Indian Institute of Science(IISc) where I led the development of telepresence systems for humanoid robots under the supervision of Dr Amrutur Bharadwaj. I also got to work on some cool projects like the animation of an avatar’s facial expressions and real-time viseme detection from live streams. Notably, I was also part of the team from IISc that made it to the semi-finals of the $10 MILLION Avatar XPRIZE Competition.

Outside of research, I love to bake and paint. My canvas includes but is not limited to paper, walls, rocks, my nails and my face. I also do tricks on my jump rope and hula hoop. Sadly, a wrist injury keeps me from continuing to train in aerial silks at the moment.

Recent Projects

Listen to your heart

Applying eXplainable AI techniques to compare popular neural network model architechtures and determine whether they make decisions based on the same features.. The goal is to understand what features, or time-segments, are deemed most importtant in determining whether a person has congestive heart failure.

Pandemic Prevention Strategy Testing

Leveraging Large Language Models to allow policymakers to test strategies to handle epidemic outbreaks. Comparing models trained with word embeddings from different tokenizers to determine the amount of extra information or context inferred. Additionally, testing if this extra context corrresponds to any biases.
